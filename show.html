The Company Drums&Boots

Projects the culture of Argentina. Argentina is a country located in South America.
The folkloric roots of Argentina begin around the 17th century, a product of the cultural clash of different cultures, mainly from Spain with the aborigines residing in the territory. Giving as a result of the cultural mix a unique and powerful cultural profile.
Today we bring to the stage the characteristic elements of projected dance, to result in a highly adaptable product to the different needs of shows worldwide.
Bombo, a characteristic instrument of the region, traditionally built with a hollowed-out trunk, with patches of animal skin. Used to accompany and set the rhythm for dancers and musicians. Today they take to the stage, and together with their powerful percussion, they transmit to the public a feeling of impact that will be forever in their memories.
Boleadora , a ball located at the end of a rope, was traditionally used as a weapon for fighting and hunting large animals. Today it is staged to perform complicated and risky percussive movements that mark rhythms and melodies of great impact to the expectant public.
Malambo , a dance where dancers produce melodies by striking their feet against the floor, using the tips, heels and flexing of their feet. A powerful and unique display of skill. Drums&Boots takes up its origins and reformulates this dance to bring from its ancestors a new and energetic product where body percussion is incorporated, implemented with current music.
